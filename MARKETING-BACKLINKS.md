## 3. QUORA — câu trả lời mẫu (helpful-first)

> Tìm câu hỏi đang có sẵn, trả lời thật chất lượng, link tự nhiên ở cuối. Đừng spam.

**Q: "How many calories should I eat to lose weight?"**
```
The honest answer is: it depends on your maintenance calories (TDEE), then you eat below that.

Two steps:
1. Find your TDEE — the calories you burn per day. It's based on your age, sex, height, weight, and activity level. The Mifflin-St Jeor formula is the most accurate one for most people.
2. Subtract for a deficit. A 500 kcal/day deficit ≈ 1 lb of fat loss per week; 1,000 kcal/day ≈ 2 lbs. Going below ~1,200 (women) or ~1,500 (men) usually isn't sustainable and can backfire.

So if your TDEE is 2,200, eating ~1,700/day targets about 1 lb/week.

I used this free TDEE calculator to get the number (it uses Mifflin-St Jeor and shows the deficit targets directly): https://healthcalculation.com/calorie-tdee-calculator

Recalculate every 10–15 lbs since your needs drop as you lose weight.
```

**Q: "How do I calculate my macros?"**
```
Macros = how your daily calories split into protein, carbs, and fat.

Quick method:
1. Get your daily calorie target (TDEE ± deficit/surplus).
2. Protein: ~0.7–1g per lb of body weight (higher if you lift).
3. Fat: ~0.3–0.4g per lb.
4. Carbs: fill the remaining calories (protein & carbs = 4 cal/g, fat = 9 cal/g).

Example at 1,800 cal, 150 lb: ~135g protein, ~50g fat, ~190g carbs.

If you'd rather skip the math, this free macro calculator does it from your goal (cut/maintain/bulk): https://healthcalculation.com/macro-calculator
```
