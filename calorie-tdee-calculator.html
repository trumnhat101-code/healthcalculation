How to use this calorie calculator

Enter your sex, age, weight, height and activity level. The tool estimates your BMR (calories at rest) and TDEE (total daily burn), then shows calorie targets for losing, maintaining or gaining weight.

BMR & TDEE formula (Mifflin-St Jeor)

BMR = 10·kg + 6.25·cm − 5·age + s  (s = +5 men, −161 women)

TDEE = BMR × activity factor (1.2 sedentary … 1.9 very active)

A 500-calorie daily deficit ≈ ~1 lb fat loss per week. Keep protein high and add strength training to preserve muscle.

Frequently asked questions

Should I eat back exercise calories?

Activity is already included in your TDEE factor, so you usually don't add exercise calories on top.

How low is too low?

Very aggressive deficits are hard to sustain and can cost muscle. A moderate deficit (10–20%) is more sustainable.

Is Mifflin-St Jeor accurate?

It's the most accurate estimate for most people, but it's still an estimate — adjust based on 2–3 weeks of real results.
